About NCC

The National Cadet Corps (NCC)

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the youth wing of the Indian Armed Forces, established on July 16, 1948. It aims to develop discipline, leadership, and patriotism among students. Operating under the Ministry of Defence, NCC offers voluntary training to school and college students across Army, Navy, and Air Force wings.

Motto

"Unity and Discipline" (Ekta aur Anushasan)

Objectives

  • Instill character, courage, discipline, and leadership.
  • Promote national integration and social responsibility.
  • Create a pool of trained youth ready for defense and civil services.

Key Benefits

  • Skill Development: Leadership, teamwork, and resilience.
  • Career Boost: Direct entry into Armed Forces for 'C' Certificate holders.
  • Scholarships & Incentives: Reserved seats in universities and government jobs.

Activities & Training

  • Parade drills, weapon training, and first aid.
  • Adventure sports: Trekking, rock climbing, and parasailing.
  • National Camps: Republic Day Camp (RDC), Thal Sainik Camp (TSC), and Youth Exchange Programs.
  • Military Attachments: Training with Army, Navy, and Air Force units.
