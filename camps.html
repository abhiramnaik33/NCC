Basic Training

1. Drill

Cadets must be made to understand the aim and purpose of teaching drill. Emphasis will be laid on correct bearing, marching, saluting and arms drill. Inter-squad competitions may be organized to create interest.

2. Weapon Training

Cadets take keen interest in weapon training and firing. Units must liaise with nearby Service/Para-Military Units to ensure all cadets get an opportunity to fire. Use of simulators is encouraged.

3. Adventure Training

Adventure activities develop leadership traits. These are:

  • Land - Mountaineering, Rock Climbing, Trekking
  • Water - Sailing, Rafting, Scuba Diving
  • Air - Parasailing

4. Youth Exchange Programme

Conducted with 10 countries to foster international awareness and understanding. Partner countries include Australia, Bangladesh, Bhutan, Canada, Nepal, Singapore, etc.
